The Present

After Abandoning my Food Project in the third month of the semester, I went back to focusing on school, the contrast from pursuing several projects at once to going back to the school pace made school seem boring. I began acing my classes and being the most experienced programmer in my college classes with essentialy no effort. I had competitors earlier in my first years of college, but the gap created by experience was too large, I could feel the distance from my classmates. I don't remember most of the events of the spring 2021 semester since it was so uninteresting. But I still felt like I knew nothing, and I still felt like there was so much to learn as there is.

I've become more aware of all I don't understand and know as I learn more, It was just like the philosopher said, I trully know nothing. Though my interests make the range a lot shorter. I spent the entirety of the coming summer learning C++. I didn't know what I was missing so I studied and studied more, I truly appreciate everyone that has given me the opportunity to know so much. Including the countless youtube tutorials

I have become proficient at using C++, even moreso than Javascript, but I don't any projects large enough to add to my portfolio. I currently understand the importance of data structures, OOP, and algorithms, and I'm finding ways to learn more.

FOOD!
My most interesting Project!

The motivation to create this game was boredom, working with HTML and css is fine, but Javascript had always been far more engaging, every lesson I took regarding Javascript stuck to my head far more frequently than its webDev counter-parts. aking videogames was my original motivation for attempting this lifestyle, and this; though incomplete was trullly a fun time, I learned a lot about programming from this project alone. I've come to understand and appreciate Servers and web design, but no preject had ever been as engaging.

I created this text-based game with a really AGILE mindset, though I didn't know what AGILE was at the time, It was just really bad planning, the potentional bugs are very high, though I've dealt with most of them. I gave up when I realized the exponential nature of the growth of bugs per each update. I am really interested in games like this, and will be properly implementing it after I graduate as a personal project.
This game will never be completed, at least not as in this language, Javascript is fine, but It truly can not hold a candle to C++
